Hypothesis Testing Why?

Hypothesis testing helps us:

Validate assumptions with statistical evidence

Avoid decisions based only on visual trends

Provide confidence-backed insights

1.Does fuel type affect car price?

Problem Statement

Do Petrol and Diesel cars have significantly different average prices?

Hypotheses

Null Hypothesis (H): Mean price of Petrol cars = Mean price of Diesel cars

Alternative Hypothesis (H): Mean price of Petrol cars Mean price of Diesel cars

Test Used

Independent Two-Sample t-Test

In[25]:
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind

petrol = df[df['Fuel'] == 'Petrol']['Price']
diesel = df[df['Fuel'] == 'Diesel']['Price']

t_stat, p_value = ttest_ind(petrol, diesel, equal_var=False)

print("T-statistic:", t_stat)
print("P-value:", p_value)

T-statistic: 3.060614839038217
P-value: 0.00279785007922424

Decision Rule

If p < 0.05 Reject H

If p 0.05 Fail to reject H

Business Insight

If rejected, fuel type significantly impacts pricing pricing models should consider fuel type.

2. Is Km_Driven related to price?

Hypotheses

H: Km_Driven has no impact on car price

H: Km_Driven significantly impacts car price

Test Used

Pearson Correlation Test

In[29]:
from scipy.stats import pearsonr

corr, p_value = pearsonr(df['Km_Driven'], df['Price'])

print("Correlation:", corr)
print("P-value:", p_value)

Correlation: -0.43043842693936935
P-value: 1.795247314285117e-19

Decision Rule

If p < 0.05 Reject H

If p 0.05 Fail to reject H

Insight

Strong negative correlation depreciation is a key pricing factor. There is a statistically significant negative relationship between Km_Driven and resale price, confirming that depreciation plays a major role in used-car pricing.
